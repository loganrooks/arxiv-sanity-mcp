# Same-Vendor Adversarial Audit — v0.2 Plan + VISION + LONG-ARC + METHODOLOGY

## Hostile summary

The author committed v0.2 *and* wrote VISION + LONG-ARC + the METHODOLOGY practice-disciplines extension in a single session, and shipped the lot before the paired audit they themselves enshrined as a discipline. The artifacts pass surface-level calibration: hedges appear in "Risks," "Open vision questions," and "Reopen conditions." But the load-bearing prose is studded with the very rhetorical labels the author flagged as a recurring failure mode — "load-bearing," "binding," "first-class," "canonical" — and several of those labels do argument-shaping work without argument under them. The Option A/B/C framing in the deliberation that grounds ADR-0005 is reverse-engineered toward Option B in two specific moves I identify below; those moves are not visible from inside ADR-0005 itself. Tournament narrowing has been ejected by name and reintroduced by structure: lens "default behavior" when `lens=` is omitted is unspecified in the architectural commit, and the longitudinal pilot's capture schema (LPILOT-01/02) implicitly tournaments lenses by recording "lens-of-record per event." The methodology extension is the cleanest piece of work in the set, but it has expanded a previously stable interpretive document into an operational one and discipline F (pattern-watch with self-application) functions more as philosophical seal than operational protocol.

## Findings by dimension

### Dimension A — Closure pressure recurrence

**Finding A1 — "Load-bearing" used as rhetorical label without argument under it.** *Confidence: medium-high.*

`VISION.md:34` introduces a header titled "Load-bearing surface: the multi-lens substrate." The body at `VISION.md:36` then states: "*The architecturally load-bearing claim of this platform is that no single retrieval lens can serve practicing AI research.*" The argument given in the same sentence is descriptive: AI research is "intensely community-structured… methodologically diverse… rapidly paradigm-shifting." Each of those properties might motivate a *citation* lens or a *methodological* lens individually, but none of them argue *that no single lens can suffice* — that is a stronger claim than the cited properties license. A single sufficiently-rich lens (a fused embedding over citation, methodology, and topic features) would absorb all three properties without violating them. The argument-form is "research has property X, therefore single lens is insufficient" without bridging premises. "Load-bearing" is the label that holds the argument together where the argument is thin.

The same pattern appears at `LONG-ARC.md:46-52`, where six "anti-patterns" are listed each with **Counter-posture** in bold. The form is diagnostic-then-prescription, but the counter-postures (e.g., `LONG-ARC.md:46` "rank-and-deprioritize, not eliminate") are stated as solutions to the diagnostic. Neither the diagnostic nor the counter-posture is given evidence that *this* counter-posture would have prevented the drift. "Counter-posture: X" is doing the same load-bearing-label work as "load-bearing" itself.

**For this finding to dissolve:** the author would have to show either (a) that "single lens cannot serve" follows from the properties named, with bridging premises stated, or (b) that "load-bearing" is being used registrally rather than argumentatively (in which case the *header* and the *opening clause* are doing the inflation, not the body).

**Finding A2 — Calibrated language confined to closing sections.** *Confidence: high.*

METHODOLOGY discipline D (`METHODOLOGY.md:139-148`) explicitly diagnoses the pattern of confidence calibration "confined to a closing footnote" while prose above uses unhedged inflation. The audit target documents replicate exactly this structure:

- `VISION.md` runs unhedged through 93 lines of declarative prose ("The platform's job is to support that practice — not to replace judgment with ranked lists" at `:22`; "*no single retrieval lens can serve practicing AI research*" at `:36`). The first calibration-flavored language appears at `:94-102` ("Open vision questions") and `:106-112` ("Reopen conditions"). That is exactly the pattern discipline D names.
- `LONG-ARC.md:23-41` runs declarative through "Protected seams" and "Anti-patterns to detect" with no in-prose calibration. The first hedges arrive at `:64-75` ("Explicit non-decisions") and `:106-115` ("Reopen conditions"). Same pattern.
- `ADR-0005`'s Decision section (`:22-38`) is unhedged commitment. Calibration appears at `:48-51` ("Negative" consequences) and `:54-63` ("Notes" / "What this ADR explicitly does not commit").

The *placement* of calibration is precisely what discipline D forbids — at the foot of the document, after the load-bearing prose has done its framing work. This is a discipline-D violation by the document that authored discipline D.

**For this finding to dissolve:** the author would have to argue that "Open vision questions," "Explicit non-decisions," and "Reopen conditions" are structurally different from a closing footnote because they are headed sections with semantic load. That argument has merit but does not address the asymmetry: there is no analogous in-prose hedge at the *site* of each load-bearing claim.

**Finding A3 — Multi-item commitments listed without per-item credence.** *Confidence: high.*

The prompt explicitly flagged this pattern. `v0.2-MILESTONE.md:14-20` lists seven architectural commitments under "Architectural commitments (from ADR-0005)" — Lens interface, lens= parameter, ProfileRankingService dispatcher, bundle-of-signals primitive, citations storage, generalized SearchResult, lens-disagreement and intersection. Of these seven, the property audit (`audits/2026-04-25-phase-3-property-audit-opus.md`) gives direct verification only for the bundle-of-signals primitive ("extensible" — `signal_type` open at DB level). The other six are inferred consequences of the architectural commit. The list does not distinguish "verified extensible" (1) from "inferred consequence we are confident about" (2-3) from "speculative until built" (4-7). All seven appear in the same indexed-bullet form. The reader cannot tell which items are evidentially anchored.

**For this finding to dissolve:** the milestone document would need to mark items by ground (verified / inferred / speculative). The current presentation reads as one-item-equals-one-item.

### Dimension B — Reverse-engineered necessity

**Finding B1 — Option A is straw-manned to make B inevitable.** *Confidence: medium-high.*

ADR-0005 Context (`:19`) reads: "*Option A (full multi-lens substrate, 3-4 mo) is overscoped given Property 1's verdict — the profile primitive doesn't need rewriting.*" This is the entire argument against A. Notice what it does: it conflates "full multi-lens substrate" (option A) with "profile primitive rewrite." A full multi-lens substrate could ship the lens abstraction without rewriting the profile primitive. The Property audit ruling that the primitive is extensible *removes a cost* from A; it does not make A overscoped. Yet the ADR uses it to *eliminate* A.

The Option C dismissal at the same location is similarly thin: "*Option C (refactor + 1 lens, ~1 mo) under-tests the abstraction: a single-lens 'interface' risks shipping shaped exactly to the lens you happen to build.*" This is a real concern (LONG-ARC names it as an anti-pattern at `:47`), but it is also exactly the pattern of constructing a danger to make the alternative inevitable. There exist mitigations for "single-lens-interface-by-accident" short of shipping a second lens — design walkthroughs (which Phase 15 plan 3 itself uses), API review with a hypothetical second lens, sketching the second lens's contract without implementing. Option C augmented with a design walkthrough for a second lens is an unconsidered fourth option that the three-option framing forecloses.

The framing has the structure: "A is too much, C is too little, therefore B." The middle option is the only sensible one *because the option space was constructed that way.* This is the reverse-engineered-necessity pattern from the 2026-04-16 deliberation review, and it recurs here.

**For this finding to dissolve:** the author would have to show that (a) Option A's overscope concern is independent of the profile primitive verdict, or (b) Option C's risk cannot be mitigated short of shipping a second lens. Neither is shown in ADR-0005.

**Finding B2 — "Validate the abstraction by shipping at least two lenses" is methodological commitment dressed as empirical claim.** *Confidence: medium.*

`VISION.md:38`: "*Lens architecture must be validated by use, not by design — the abstraction is most likely to be right when at least two lenses are shipped against it.*" The hedge ("most likely") is gentle. But the underlying claim — that a *second implementation is the validating use* — is not empirical. It is a methodological commitment about what counts as validation. There is no evidence cited that two implementations validate an abstraction better than one implementation plus a thorough design walkthrough. The hostile read: this is a stipulation that justifies the cost of shipping the second lens, not a finding that demands it.

**For this finding to dissolve:** the author would need to point to evidence (in this codebase or in the literature) that two-implementation validation outperforms one-implementation-plus-walkthrough validation. Absent that, the claim is an asserted preference.

**Finding B3 — The six practice disciplines are not derived from the audit cycle as stated.** *Confidence: medium.*

`METHODOLOGY.md:99` opens: "*Added 2026-04-25 after the methodology-audit cycle (pressure pass + paired AI review + operationalization audit + property audit). The six lenses above are interpretive — they govern how a finding should be read. The disciplines below are operational.*" The implication: each discipline is a lesson from the audit cycle. Examining the disciplines:

- A (paired review): grounded — directly motivated by the pressure pass overreach the paired review caught.
- B (model verification before delegation): grounded — directly motivated by the Property audit Explore-vs-Opus flip.
- C (single-reader factual claims need verification): grounded — same Property audit incident.
- D (calibrated language): grounded — overstep audit found 13 instances.
- E (pressure-test artifacts before remedies): grounded — 2026-04-16 deliberation review pattern.
- F (pattern-watch with self-application): *the audit cycle did not produce a finding requiring a meta-discipline;* the discipline reflects an awareness *the author had during composition of the others*, not a lesson the cycle's evidence produced.

Discipline F is post-hoc rationalization of the document's own structural completeness, not a lesson from the audit. It is the seventh-item-that-makes-the-list-feel-complete pattern. The hostile read is that the author noticed that disciplines A-E were corrective and discipline F was added to make the list philosophically reflexive.

**For this finding to dissolve:** point to the specific artifact in the audit cycle whose evidence required a self-application meta-discipline. I do not find one.

### Dimension C — Tournament-narrowing / fusion-by-default recurrence

**Finding C1 — `lens=` default behavior is unspecified, leaving silent-default risk.** *Confidence: high.*

The ADR-0005 architectural commit at `:31` says: "*lens= parameter on the four discovery MCP tools (`search_papers`, `browse_recent`, `find_related_papers`, `get_paper`), defaulting to a configurable preferred lens or to 'all available.'*" The disjunction here is doing concealment work. "A configurable preferred lens" *is* a silent default in the LONG-ARC sense (`:48`): "*A reference frame… becomes the implicit baseline that all alternatives are measured against, even when nothing argued for it being baseline.*" "Or to 'all available'" is the alternative, but it is not explicitly committed.

The milestone clarifies at `v0.2-MILESTONE.md:15`: "*Default to semantic lens for backward compatibility.*" This is the silent default. It is justified by backward-compat, which is reasonable, but it is not flagged as an instance of the very pattern LONG-ARC anti-patterns name. The semantic lens becomes the implicit baseline by default; new users who do not specify `lens=` get semantic-only results; the multi-lens substrate ships in single-lens-by-default form.

The hostile read: tournament narrowing has been excluded *as a methodology* but a softer version of the same pathology — incumbent-as-default — has been smuggled back in under "backward compatibility."

The further hostile question: when a caller passes `lens=["semantic", "citation_community"]` with no operation specified (no `mode=intersection` or `mode=disagreement`), what happens? The ROADMAP Phase 16 (`:336`) describes operations as "either a dedicated tool, a `mode=disagreement` parameter… or a composable filter; design choice recorded with rationale." The default of `lens=[A, B]` without operation is unspecified. Implementers will pick a default at integration time. The most likely default — return papers from both, deduped, sorted by some composite — *is fusion-by-default*, exactly what the plan proscribes.

**For this finding to dissolve:** the architectural commit would need to specify default behavior explicitly. It does not.

**Finding C2 — Phase 17 capture schema implicitly tournament-frames the pilot.** *Confidence: medium-high.*

`REQUIREMENTS.md:129` LPILOT-02: "*Harness captures triage events with timestamps: selection, dismissal, return-to-paper, lens-of-record per event.*" "Lens-of-record per event" is the structural smuggle. It assumes each event has *a* lens-of-record — the lens that produced the paper that was triaged. But for multi-lens queries (intersection, disagreement, agreement), the *paper has multiple lens-of-records.* The capture schema as specified will force assignment of one lens to each event, which structurally re-introduces winner-pick framing at the analysis layer.

The downstream consequence is exactly the failure mode `008` exhibited: at analysis time, the question becomes "which lens led to the most selections" — i.e., which lens won. The pilot is not protected from tournament framing by being labeled "longitudinal pilot"; the capture schema determines what questions it can answer, and the schema as written can answer winner-pick questions naturally and multi-lens questions only awkwardly.

**For this finding to dissolve:** LPILOT-02 would need to specify that capture preserves *all* lenses that surfaced a paper, not "lens-of-record per event."

**Finding C3 — Semantic-lens embedding model is a silent v0.1-baseline carry-forward.** *Confidence: medium.*

`v0.2-MILESTONE.md:14`: "*Existing semantic lens — the current TF-IDF + lexical + workflow-state stack, formalized as a registered lens with explicit per-lens scoring.*" The embedding model selection question, which the spike program 005-008 spent four spikes on, is not addressed at the v0.2 architectural commit layer. The semantic lens just inherits the v0.1 baseline (TF-IDF + lexical). This is fine *as a Phase 12 scope decision*, but it is not flagged as a deferred decision; it appears as a settled architecture. The 2026-03-30 deliberation that produced spikes 005-008 viewed embedding-model selection as the central design call. v0.2 retroactively decides "the embedding model question is not v0.2 work" without a decision document.

The LONG-ARC anti-pattern at `:51` is explicit: "*Embedding-model choice as load-bearing decision.*" The counter-posture there is "*treat embedding-model selection as one lens-design decision among many.*" v0.2 does treat it that way — by inheriting it silently. This may be the right call, but it is not visible as a call.

**For this finding to dissolve:** v0.2 would need to add a "deferred decisions" entry stating that the semantic-lens embedding-model choice is held over to a later milestone, with reasons. The closest existing item is `LONG-ARC.md:69` which discusses fusion strategy, not embedding-model choice.

### Dimension D — Self-application gaps

**Finding D1 — The author committed v0.2 before the paired audit they prescribed.** *Confidence: high.*

Discipline A (`METHODOLOGY.md:101-114`) requires paired review for "audits or critiques whose framing claims are load-bearing on remedies." ADR-0005 *is* the remedy. v0.2-MILESTONE.md ratifies it. The discipline says "Audit-level remedies do not get adopted from single-reader output if the framing is contestable" (`:113`). ADR-0005's framing is contestable (see Dimension B). The author preempts this critique at `v0.2-MILESTONE.md:96` by binding to METHODOLOGY, but binding-by-citation is not the same as following the discipline. The right order would have been: deliberation → paired audit on the deliberation → ADR after audit incorporated. The actual order was: deliberation → ADR → paired audit on the ADR. The discipline did not bind its own author at the moment it was authored.

**For this finding to dissolve:** the author would need to show the v0.2 plan and ADR are explicitly provisional pending this audit's output, with a commitment to revise on findings. The current frontmatter says "Status: Accepted" (`ADR-0005:3`) and "Status: Planned (kicked off 2026-04-25)" (`v0.2-MILESTONE.md:3`). Neither says "provisional pending audit."

**Finding D2 — OpenAlex coverage spike is not pre-registered with thresholds.** *Confidence: medium-high.*

`v0.2-MILESTONE.md:79`: "*Phase 14's first plan includes a coverage spike to characterize this on the 126-paper corpus before committing to storage shape.*" `ROADMAP.md:299` makes this Phase 14 plan 1's success criterion: "*OpenAlex coverage spike completes: edge density per paper, freshness profile, missingness pattern characterized for the 126-paper corpus; results recorded in a spike artifact.*"

What thresholds determine "OpenAlex coverage is sufficient for v0.2 lens-validation"? The artifacts do not say. METHODOLOGY discipline B and the broader Bayesian-updating lens (`:32-40`) require pre-registration: "*State a prior probability before execution and track how the evidence shifts it.*" The Phase 14 spike is a gating decision (storage shape commitment depends on it), but the gating thresholds are not pre-registered. The hostile read: at execution time, *whatever* coverage the spike finds will be deemed sufficient, because no pre-committed threshold exists to falsify sufficiency.

**For this finding to dissolve:** Phase 14 plan 1 would need to specify pre-registered thresholds (e.g., "if median paper has <K cited-by edges, defer citation lens; if missingness > X% on AI/CS papers post-2023, switch to Semantic Scholar").

**Finding D3 — Calibration bound to closing sections in the audit-target documents themselves.** *Confidence: high.* (See Finding A2; restated here as a self-application gap.)

**For this finding to dissolve:** the author would have to argue (a) sectioned calibration ≠ closing-footnote calibration, or (b) declarative load-bearing prose is acceptable when the document type warrants it (VISION as proclamation, ADR as commit). ### Dimension F — Methodology extension scope-creep

**Finding F1 — Center of gravity has shifted from interpretive lenses to operational protocols.** *Confidence: medium-high.*

The pre-extension METHODOLOGY.md (`:1-95`) was a 95-line document with six interpretive lenses, each with diagnose/recommend/concrete-change/when-it-catches structure. The added six disciplines (`:97-164`, ~67 lines) bring the document to 183 lines. More importantly, the disciplines are *operational* — they specify what to do procedurally (paired review, model verification, file:line citations, calibrated register). The original lenses were *interpretive* — they specified what to look for, not what to do.

The hostile read: the document's purpose has shifted from "lenses for thinking about findings" to "protocols for producing findings." The shift is consequential. Future spike designers reading the document will face a longer, more procedural standing reference. The risk is bureaucratic accretion: the document becomes a checklist, not a thinking aid.

`METHODOLOGY.md:181-183` has a "What this document cannot do" section that flags the checklist risk for the lenses. It does not flag the checklist risk for the practice disciplines, even though they are far more checklist-prone.

**For this finding to dissolve:** the document would need to either (a) split disciplines into a separate file, or (b) add a "What this document cannot do" item flagging that the disciplines should not be applied as a checklist.

**Finding F2 — Discipline F is meta-discipline that adds no operational guidance the others don't already imply.** *Confidence: medium.*

Discipline F at `METHODOLOGY.md:158-164`: "*When auditing a layer below, audit also the audit's own susceptibility to the pattern. Self-application is part of the discipline, not optional polish. Concrete change: Methodology-audit artifacts include a self-application section explicitly addressing whether the audit itself exhibits what it diagnoses.*"

The hostile read: F exists because discipline F's *absence* would feel like a gap given the document's own theme (recurring patterns at every layer), not because F catches what A-E miss.

**For this finding to dissolve:** point to a finding that would be caught only by discipline F's self-application requirement and missed by A-E. The author does not provide an example.

## Steelman residue

I want to be honest about which adversarial frames did less work than they look like they did on first writing.

The *Option A is straw-manned* frame (Finding B1) is real but partial. The author *did* have grounds to deprioritize Option A — engineering-cost is a legitimate consideration, and "3-4 mo for a 2-mo target" is a defensible reason regardless of whether the profile primitive needs rewriting. My attack lands on the *form* of the dismissal (using the property verdict to eliminate A rather than to soften it) but not on the *direction* of the dismissal. If the deliberation document has the cost argument elsewhere, my frame weakens. I did not read the deliberation in full; I read ADR-0005's compression of it. The compression *did* construct the option space tightly, but the underlying cost reasoning may be more defensible than the compression suggests.

The *load-bearing as rhetorical label* frame (Finding A1) is the strongest of my closure-pressure attacks but partly self-undermining. "Load-bearing" is the project's vernacular vocabulary by 2026-04-25 — the author has used it for months, the audit cycle uses it, the cross-vendor reviewer uses it. Asking the author to retire the label because it is *used as a label* would make the project's own working vocabulary impossible. The honest residue is that the *header* "Load-bearing surface" at `VISION.md:34` and the *opening clause* "*The architecturally load-bearing claim*" at `:36` do framing work the body needs to support but doesn't quite. That is a smaller finding than "load-bearing is rhetorical inflation throughout."

The *capture schema tournament-frames the pilot* frame (Finding C2) is real but the remedy is small. "Lens-of-record" can be replaced with "lenses-that-surfaced-the-paper" without redesigning Phase 17. The structural concern is real, but it does not undermine the longitudinal pilot as a category — it requires a one-line specification fix in LPILOT-02. I overstated this finding's reach by saying "the pilot is not protected from tournament framing." The pilot framework *is* protected; the capture schema needs adjustment.

The *discipline F is meta-philosophical* frame (Finding F2) is the weakest of my methodology-extension attacks. Discipline F may be epistemically less informative than A-E, but it has rhetorical-pedagogical value: future readers of the document benefit from being told "the pattern recurs at every layer including this one" even if no specific protocol attaches. The framing-as-philosophical-seal critique is real, but the argument that F adds *nothing* over A-E is too strong — F adds a reminder, which has value in a document that is itself trying to be remembered.

The *anti-vision under hostile read* frames (E1-E3) are real but the relevant question is what level of argumentation a VISION document is supposed to have. Vision documents are constitutive — they declare identity, they do not argue it. The author's choice to assert "not arxiv-sanity-but-better" without arguing it may be appropriate for the document type. The hostile frame "this is asserted, not argued" applies to a kind of argumentation VISION may not be obligated to provide. The residue is that the *anti-vision* section specifically sits inside a document the author labeled "living — refine as the substrate matures" (`VISION.md:3`); claims labeled living should be hedge-able in their own prose, and they aren't.

## What survives

After the steelman pass, the findings that survive and warrant the v0.2 plan author's response:

1. **The `lens=` default behavior is unspecified at the architectural-commit layer (Finding C1).** This is concrete, fixable, and important. Specify in ADR-0005 or v0.2-MILESTONE.md: (a) what the default is when `lens=` is omitted; (b) what the default operation is when `lens=[A,B]` is passed without `mode=`; (c) if the default for case (a) is "semantic," flag it as a backward-compat-driven silent default and bind it to a sunset clause (e.g., "after Phase 17 pilot, evaluate whether default should change").

2. **The Phase 17 capture schema needs to preserve multi-lens attribution (Finding C2).** Replace LPILOT-02's "lens-of-record per event" with "lens or lenses that surfaced the paper, with per-lens contribution tracked." This is a one-line specification fix.

3. **The Option A/B/C framing in ADR-0005 should be steelmanned, especially Option C plus walkthroughs (Finding B1).** The author's response could be either (a) extend ADR-0005 with a paragraph on why Option C-plus-design-walkthrough is insufficient, or (b) acknowledge that B was selected on a defensible cost-and-validation argument that did not require Option A and Option C to be implausible.

4. **The OpenAlex coverage spike needs pre-registered thresholds (Finding D2).** Phase 14 plan 1 should specify what counts as "sufficient" coverage *before* execution, per disciplines B and the Bayesian lens. Otherwise the plan invites a post-hoc declaration of sufficiency.

5. **The semantic-lens embedding-model choice should be flagged as deferred (Finding C3).** Add to LONG-ARC's "Explicit non-decisions" list: "Semantic-lens embedding-model selection (TF-IDF baseline carries forward to v0.2; revisit at v0.3 or earlier if pilot signals warrant)."

6. **The author's own discipline-A application is the most consequential self-application gap (Finding D1).** The right move now is the audit's own existence, but the v0.2 documents should be marked as adjustable based on this audit's findings — not as "Accepted" full stop.

7. **METHODOLOGY discipline F should be either operationalized or labeled as meta-discipline (Finding F2).** Either specify what F catches that A-E don't, or rename F as a "register-marking item" rather than a discipline with its own concrete-change clause.

8. **Calibration that runs through prose, not just at closing sections, is the unfinished discipline-D self-application (Finding A2 / D3).** The four documents should add in-prose hedges at load-bearing claims — particularly `VISION.md:34-38` where "load-bearing" claims sit unhedged at the document's argumentative center.

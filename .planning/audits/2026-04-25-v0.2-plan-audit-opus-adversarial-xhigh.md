## Summary

The v0.2 plan is structurally sound and grounded in real evidence (ADR-0005 + property audit). The strongest survived findings concern (a) silent-default surfaces around `lens=` semantics that the plan proscribes by name but does not close by mechanism — particularly the unspecified behavior when multiple lenses are passed and the unspecified default-lens identity (`v0.2-MILESTONE.md:15` says "default to semantic lens for backward compatibility"; ADR-0005:31 says "defaulting to a configurable preferred lens or to 'all available'" — the two artifacts disagree); (b) the longitudinal pilot's "lens-of-record per event" capture field structurally re-introduces the winner-pick pattern at analysis time even when the plan's prose ejects it; (c) the six methodology practice-disciplines lean toward a closed taxonomy of failure modes the team has already encountered, with discipline F (pattern-watch with self-application) doing meta-discipline work that may collapse into the others rather than adding orthogonal guidance; and (d) the milestone document's success-criteria table conflates verified, planned, and aspirational items in a single indexed list. The most consequential strength is the property-audit-grounded option selection: the chain from migration-005 verification → property verdicts → Option B selection → ADR-0005 → milestone is the team's strongest example of evidence-anchored planning, and the v0.2 plan honors that anchoring at the structural level even where its prose drifts.

## Findings by dimension

### Dimension A — Closure pressure recurrence

**A1. "Default to semantic lens for backward compatibility" vs "configurable preferred lens or 'all available'": closure-by-rephrasing across artifacts.**

- **What:** `v0.2-MILESTONE.md:15` says the `lens=` parameter on the four discovery tools "Default[s] to semantic lens for backward compatibility." `ADR-0005:31` says it defaults "to a configurable preferred lens or to 'all available.'" These are not the same default, and the milestone reads as if it has settled what ADR-0005 explicitly leaves open. `REQUIREMENTS.md:134` (MCP-08) says only "default preserves v0.1 behavior" — a third formulation. None of the three documents acknowledges the disagreement.
- **Why it matters:** Ground 4 (methodology discipline D — calibrated language as default register) and Ground 5 (delivery risk — silent defaults at integration time). The author wrote both documents in the same session (per the dispatch context). The disagreement was either (i) a deliberate refinement that should have been called out as an ADR-0005 amendment, or (ii) closure-by-rephrasing where the milestone document quietly tightened an open ADR question. Either way, the on-disk inconsistency will cost the implementer a re-deliberation when they hit it. Phase 12 plan 1 will inherit whichever default the implementer reads first.
- **Severity tier:** quality (could be blocking if "all available" is the default — that's a fan-out cost the team has not budgeted).
- **Confidence:** high on the textual disagreement; medium on the closure-pressure read (the gap is small enough to be an editorial slip, but the milestone document is the operational artifact and the ADR is the architectural artifact, so the gap matters).
- **What would dissolve:** A note in the milestone (or an ADR-0005 erratum) saying "default behavior = semantic lens; 'all available' considered and deferred because [reason]." Or the inverse, with reasoning.
- **Suggested improvement direction:** Add one sentence to the v0.2 milestone "Open scoping calls (resolved as of 2026-04-25)" section reconciling the two: "Default lens when `lens=` is omitted: semantic (preserves v0.1 behavior). 'All available' considered as a UX-richer alternative; deferred because v0.1 callers expect single-lens response shape."

**A2. "First-class" used four times across artifacts; one usage does work the others do not.**

- **What:** `v0.2-MILESTONE.md:20` ("Lens-disagreement and intersection as MCP-exposed operations"), ADR-0005:36 (same), VISION:50 ("Per-recommendation explanation — why this paper, by which lens"), LONG-ARC:40 ("MCP-native operations... Steering, intersection, lens-disagreement, and explanation are first-class operations exposed via MCP"). The first three are descriptive (these are operations the platform exposes). The LONG-ARC usage is doing protected-seam work — it asserts these are commitments current planning must preserve. That is a stronger claim than "first-class" elsewhere.
- **Why it matters:** Ground 4 (calibrated language). The METHODOLOGY discipline D explicitly flags "rhetorical labels ('load-bearing,' 'critical,' all-caps emphasis) require argument under them." "First-class" is in the same family. In the LONG-ARC usage it does seam-protection work and the argument under it (per-lens explanation, ADR-0003 inspectability) is real. In the milestone usage it is essentially a synonym for "exposed via MCP" and adds no information.
- **Severity tier:** taste (flagged as such). I am noting it because the team's own discipline names rhetorical-label inflation; same-vendor reading should catch it. But three of the four usages are mild and harmless.
- **Confidence:** medium-high.
- **What would dissolve:** Substitute "exposed via MCP" for "first-class" where the latter is doing no extra work.
- **Suggested improvement direction:** A pass on "first-class," "load-bearing," "binding" tokens across the four artifacts, replacing those that don't carry an argument with the literal claim. The LONG-ARC usages survive on argument; the milestone/ADR usages mostly do not.

**A3. Milestone success criteria conflate verified, planned, and aspirational.**

- **What:** `v0.2-MILESTONE.md:67-75` lists seven milestone-level success criteria. Items 1, 3, 4, 5 are operational (binary verifiable post-execution). Item 2 ("Adding a hypothetical third lens... does not require modifying any existing consumer code, validated by design walkthrough") is methodological (the walkthrough is the verification, but the walkthrough itself is judgment-laden). Items 6 ("ADR-0001's coexistence commitment is honored") and 7 ("All new ranking/retrieval signals carry provenance") are claims that supervene on the others — they are not independently verifiable success criteria, they are inferences from criterion 1 + 4 + 5 + the architectural commitments.
- **Why it matters:** Ground 1 (stated end goals — what the milestone commits to deliver). Indexed-list framing equalizes claims of different epistemic kinds. A reviewer reading the criteria cannot tell which are pass/fail tests and which are interpretive judgments. METHODOLOGY discipline D's guidance applies: per-criterion credence should not be hidden behind list uniformity.
- **Severity tier:** quality.
- **Confidence:** high on the structural observation; medium on the cost (the team is small, the author understands the difference, but a future reader or a contractor would not).
- **What would dissolve:** Annotate criteria as `[operational]`, `[methodological]`, or `[supervenient]`. Or split into two sub-lists: "pass/fail criteria" and "supervenient commitments verified by the above."
- **Suggested improvement direction:** Add a status marker to each criterion (the team already uses `[chosen for now]` etc. in REQUIREMENTS.md; the convention exists).

**A4. "Tournament-narrowing pattern recurrence" risk acknowledged; mitigation deferred to anti-pattern watch.**

- **What:** `v0.2-MILESTONE.md:91`: "Tournament-narrowing pattern recurrence. The redirection is committed but the pattern is recurrent; Phase 12-15 work could quietly drift toward 'which lens wins' if not actively counter-postured. Mitigation: LONG-ARC.md anti-pattern watch; rank-and-deprioritize framing throughout."
- **Why it matters:** Ground 4 (methodology — METHODOLOGY discipline F is pattern-watch with self-application). The risk is real and the diagnosis is good, but "anti-pattern watch" is not a mechanism — it's a commitment to be vigilant. The team's own diagnosis from the 005-008 cycle is that vigilance is what failed: each step was locally defensible, the drift was invisible from inside. The mitigation is structurally the same kind of discipline that already failed.
- **Severity tier:** quality (would be blocking if the v0.2 plan had no other safeguards; it does have ADR-0005, the second-lens requirement, and the longitudinal-pilot framing — those are mechanisms).
- **Confidence:** high.
- **What would dissolve:** A concrete trigger or checkpoint for tournament-narrowing detection — e.g., "at Phase 15 plan 3, the lens-extensibility design walkthrough must explicitly check whether per-lens framing has been preserved in implementation, not just in interface." That converts vigilance into a checkpoint.
- **Suggested improvement direction:** Add a Phase-15-plan-3 or Phase-16 checkpoint criterion: "no scoring fusion is the implicit default in any new MCP path; pattern explicitly checked at this phase boundary." This makes the anti-pattern watch operational, not aspirational.

### Dimension B — Reverse-engineered necessity

**B1. The three Options (A, B, C) for the roadmap commit are presented as the option space; they are not exhaustive.**

- **What:** `ADR-0005:19` characterizes Option A as "full multi-lens substrate, 3-4 mo" (overscoped — Property 1 says profile primitive doesn't need rewriting), Option C as "refactor + 1 lens, ~1 mo" (under-tests the abstraction), and concludes "Only a second lens forces the tool surface (Property 2) and storage layer (Property 3) to actually serve two lens shapes." The argument is sound *given that the option space is A/B/C*. Plausible alternatives not listed: (i) refactor + 0 lenses (just the lens abstraction + better tests, validating that the existing semantic lens fits the abstraction shape with a paired internal review), (ii) lens abstraction + a *thin* second lens deliberately scoped to test specifically the surface and storage shape rather than to ship a usable citation experience, (iii) defer the abstraction entirely and let v0.2 ship just the citation-graph data integration as a parallel pipeline, learning from operating two pipelines before designing the abstraction.
- **Why it matters:** Ground 4 (METHODOLOGY discipline E — pressure-test artifacts before adopting remedies; "the constructed-option-space mistake recurs unless the next step is anchored in artifact reading rather than doctrine"). The team has explicitly named this exact failure mode. The deliberation document (referenced from the milestone) presumably enumerated more options; ADR-0005 retains only three. Whether (i)/(ii)/(iii) were considered and rejected, or never appeared in the option space, is invisible from the on-disk artifact.
- **Severity tier:** quality. Option B may well be the right answer; the issue is the reasoning structure, not the conclusion. The Property-audit grounding is real evidence; the option-space framing is constructed.
- **Confidence:** medium. I have not read the deliberation document (`.planning/deliberations/2026-04-25-long-arc-and-multi-lens-redirection.md`) for the same-session-contamination reason that does not apply here — I can read it. But the audit is about what the milestone+ADR commit, not about what the deliberation considered. If the deliberation explicitly considered (i)/(ii)/(iii) and ruled them out, ADR-0005 would benefit from naming them and saying so.
- **What would dissolve:** A footnote in ADR-0005's Decision section enumerating what the option space considered beyond A/B/C, and why each was rejected. Or a citation to a section of the deliberation document that does this.
- **Suggested improvement direction:** Add to ADR-0005 a "Considered and rejected" subsection listing 1-2 alternatives beyond the A/B/C frame (e.g., "Lens abstraction + zero new lenses, validated by paired internal review of the abstraction shape — rejected because internal review of an abstraction without a second implementation does not reliably catch shape errors; this is the same failure mode ADR-0005 names elsewhere").

**B2. "Validate the abstraction by shipping at least two lenses": rhetorical or methodological?**

- **What:** ADR-0005's Decision rests on the claim that an abstraction is "most likely to be right when validated by a second implementation." VISION:38 echoes this. LONG-ARC:33 lists it as a protected seam. None of the three documents distinguishes whether this is (a) an empirical claim about software engineering (we have evidence that single-implementation abstractions tend to be wrong), (b) a methodological commitment (the team has chosen to require second-implementation validation), or (c) a defensive heuristic (after the 005-008 drift, the team has burnt fingers with implicit-single-lens trajectories and wants a structural counter-posture).
- **Why it matters:** Ground 1 (stated end goals — what the milestone commits to deliver) and Ground 2 (long-term vision). The distinction matters because each reading has different implications. If (a), it should cite evidence. If (b), it is binding for v0.2 and should not be revisited until LONG-ARC reopen condition 2 fires. If (c), it should be honest that the second lens is partly there because the team needs the discipline, not because the abstraction empirically requires it.
- **Severity tier:** quality.
- **Confidence:** medium. The most charitable read — and probably the correct one — is (b) + (c) jointly: the team has chosen to require second-implementation validation as a discipline, partly informed by the 005-008 drift. But ADR-0005 reads as if it's (a), which is harder to defend.
- **What would dissolve:** A clarifying sentence in ADR-0005's Decision section: "This is a methodological commitment, not an empirical claim about software engineering. The 005-008 drift demonstrated that abstractions designed without second-implementation validation drift toward the implementation that exists; this commits the team to a counter-posture."
- **Suggested improvement direction:** Same as the dissolution. One sentence.

**B3. The six audit dimensions in METHODOLOGY's practice-disciplines extension are not closed; "pattern-watch with self-application" (F) collapses into the others.**

- **What:** METHODOLOGY.md:97-164 adds six practice disciplines (A: paired review; B: model verification; C: factual-claim verification; D: calibrated language; E: pressure-test artifacts; F: pattern-watch with self-application). Disciplines A-E are operationally distinct. Discipline F is meta — "when auditing a layer below, audit also the audit's own susceptibility to the pattern" — and the "pattern" it watches for is closure-pressure, which is what disciplines D and E address. F's recommended concrete change ("methodology-audit artifacts include a self-application section") is a structural addition, not a separate operational discipline.
- **Why it matters:** Ground 4 (the methodology document's own discipline of orthogonal-discipline naming). The METHODOLOGY document is presented as having six lenses (interpretive) + six disciplines (operational), and the symmetry is rhetorically appealing. But if F is meta-discipline rather than co-equal with A-E, the symmetry is constructed. The cost is downstream: future readers will treat F as orthogonal guidance and either over-apply it (every artifact gets a self-application section even when the layered audit structure isn't present) or under-apply it (treating "self-application" as a tax to satisfy rather than a structural requirement).
- **Severity tier:** quality. The discipline is correct; the placement is what's at issue.
- **Confidence:** medium-high.
- **What would dissolve:** F is clearly distinguished from D and E by an example where applying D and E without F would still produce the failure F catches.
- **Suggested improvement direction:** Either (i) reframe F as a meta-discipline that applies to A-E (small structural change to METHODOLOGY.md, no content loss), or (ii) provide an example where F catches what D and E miss. The second would strengthen F's standalone case if it can be done; if it cannot, (i) is the honest move.

### Dimension C — Tournament-narrowing / fusion-by-default recurrence

**C1. `lens=` parameter semantics under multi-value or omitted: unspecified, integration-time defaults will fill the gap.**

- **What:** `v0.2-MILESTONE.md:15` says `lens=` defaults to semantic; ADR-0005:31 says "configurable preferred lens or 'all available'"; REQUIREMENTS.md MCP-08 says "default preserves v0.1 behavior." None specify behavior for `lens=["semantic", "citation_community"]` (a list with no fusion strategy) or `lens=None` explicitly (different from omitted). Phase 16 covers lens-disagreement and intersection ops as separate operations — but those are scoped to deliberate disagreement/intersection requests, not to the multi-lens form of an ordinary discovery query.
- **Why it matters:** Ground 5 (delivery risk — silent-defaults anti-pattern, explicitly named in LONG-ARC:48). When a Phase 12 or 13 implementer hits `search_papers(lens=["semantic", "citation_community"])` for the first time, they will pick a default behavior (fusion via RRF? union with deduplication? per-lens result lists in the response shape?) — and that default will become the de facto API. This is exactly the silent-default mechanism the methodology names.
- **Severity tier:** blocking. ADR-0005:36 says "Fusion (RRF as default if used) is one strategy among several, not the implicit default" — but the multi-value `lens=` case forces *some* default. Without an explicit decision, fusion-by-RRF or first-lens-wins will be the integration-time choice, and it will be hard to walk back.
- **Confidence:** high.
- **What would dissolve:** Phase 13 plan 1 or 2 explicitly resolves multi-value `lens=` semantics with a decision artifact. Acceptable resolutions: (a) reject with `"multi-lens via lens= requires explicit operation parameter"`, (b) return per-lens result lists as a dict keyed by lens name (no fusion), (c) explicit fusion strategy parameter required, etc. The point is the resolution is an artifact decision, not an implementation default.
- **Suggested improvement direction:** Add to Phase 13 success criteria: "Multi-value `lens=` semantics is decided with rationale: either rejected with a clear error pointing users to the disagreement/intersection operations, returned as per-lens result dict, or requires an explicit composition parameter. The decision is recorded as part of the phase artifact."

**C2. Semantic-lens embedding model not addressed; v0.1 baseline ships by silent inheritance.**

- **What:** `v0.2-MILESTONE.md` and ADR-0005 both formalize the existing semantic lens. Neither addresses the embedding model that lens uses. The Property audit (`audits/2026-04-25-phase-3-property-audit-opus.md:24`) confirms the current ranking pipeline is TF-IDF + lexical + workflow-state — there is no actual embedding model in the v0.1 semantic lens. So the "semantic lens" is currently a TF-IDF lens; the name carries forward an aspirational meaning that the implementation does not deliver.
- **Why it matters:** Ground 2 (VISION coherence). VISION:60 says "Semantic (dense embedding) | committed (v0.1, refactored in v0.2) | Currently TF-IDF + lexical baseline; semantic-model selection is per-lens design, not winner-pick." The vision is honest about it — TF-IDF is the current implementation, semantic-model selection is per-lens design. But the v0.2 plan does not include any per-lens semantic-model decision; the lens ships as TF-IDF and inherits the v0.1 naming. The risk is that this silent inheritance bakes "semantic = TF-IDF" into the architectural shape. When a later phase adds dense embeddings, it will face a choice: rename the existing lens (breaking change), or add "dense_semantic" as a separate lens, in which case the original "semantic" lens has the wrong name.
- **Severity tier:** quality. Not blocking for v0.2 delivery; quality issue for v0.3+ trajectory.
- **Confidence:** high.
- **What would dissolve:** Either (i) rename the v0.1 lens to "lexical" or "tfidf_lexical" in v0.2, treating "semantic" as a slot to fill in a future phase, or (ii) explicitly document that "semantic" is the legacy name and will be revisited when a real embedding lens ships, or (iii) commit in v0.2 to "semantic" being a label for the *family* of similarity-by-content lenses, with TF-IDF as the current member.
- **Suggested improvement direction:** Add to v0.2 milestone Open scoping calls: "Semantic lens naming. The v0.1 implementation is TF-IDF + lexical; the 'semantic' label is preserved for backward compatibility but does not yet imply dense embeddings. v0.3+ may introduce a dense-embedding lens; naming will be revisited then."

**C3. Phase 17 longitudinal pilot's "lens-of-record per event" capture field structurally re-introduces winner-pick at analysis time.**

- **What:** `ROADMAP.md:353-354`: "Capture schema designed: session-level lens usage, query log with lens(es) selected, triage events (selection, dismissal, return-to-paper), per-event lens-of-record, durable storage." `REQUIREMENTS.md:129` (LPILOT-02): "Harness captures triage events with timestamps: selection, dismissal, return-to-paper after dismissal, lens-of-record per event."
- **Why it matters:** Ground 3 (ADR-0001 + ADR-0005 — multi-lens coexistence at the implementation layer, including the analysis layer). "Lens-of-record per event" assigns a single lens identity to each user action. When a paper appears in a multi-lens result set and the user triages it, which lens gets the credit? If the schema requires one lens-of-record, the analysis question becomes "which lens is producing the most useful results" — which is winner-pick at analysis time, even though the architecture is multi-lens at retrieval time. ADR-0005:36 explicitly proscribes this pattern: "Steering, intersection, lens-disagreement, and per-paper explanation are first-class. Fusion is one strategy among several, not the implicit default." Lens-of-record is an attribution scheme, which is a form of post-hoc fusion.
- **Severity tier:** blocking. The capture schema pre-determines the analysis questions. If the schema is wrong, the four-week pilot produces winner-pick data; the team will either (a) draw winner-pick conclusions from it despite the architectural commitment against, or (b) discover at analysis time that the data does not answer the multi-lens questions and have to rebuild.
- **Confidence:** medium-high. There is a charitable read: "lens-of-record" might mean "the lens(es) that surfaced this paper in the result the user acted on" — a multi-valued field. If so, the schema is fine and the language is misleading. But REQUIREMENTS.md says "lens-of-record" (singular) and the ROADMAP language reads the same way. The risk is real even if it's resolvable by clarification.
- **What would dissolve:** Phase 17 plan 1 explicitly designs the capture schema with multi-valued lens attribution and a designated analysis pattern that does not assume one lens-per-event. Or the schema captures "lens-of-record" as the lens the user explicitly chose (if any) plus separately the lens(es) that surfaced the paper, treating the two as distinct fields.
- **Suggested improvement direction:** Replace "lens-of-record per event" with "lens(es) that surfaced the paper for this event, plus the user's explicit lens selection if any" in LPILOT-02 and ROADMAP Phase 17. Add to Phase 17 plan 1's success criteria: "the capture schema does not assume one-lens-per-event; multi-lens attribution is preserved through analysis."

### Dimension E — VISION's anti-vision under critical read

- **Why it matters:** Ground 2 (VISION coherence). Preservation-by-deferral is fragile: when the feature is built (post-v0.2), the discipline that should govern it lives only in VISION's anti-vision section. There is no architectural seam in v0.2 that would *prevent* a future implementation from sliding into silent learning. The longitudinal pilot (Phase 17) is the closest analog — it captures behavior — and `ROADMAP.md:354` does specify "Capture is opt-in / explicit; no silent profile mutation." That is good. But it applies only to the pilot harness, not to the broader profile-learning mechanism the anti-vision targets.
- **Severity tier:** quality. Not blocking for v0.2 (the feature isn't being built); quality issue for v0.3+ trajectory.
- **Confidence:** high.
- **What would dissolve:** A protected seam in LONG-ARC explicitly committing that any future behavior-derived signal mechanism must require explicit researcher confirmation, not just acknowledgment after the fact. (LONG-ARC:35 "per-lens provenance" and :40 "MCP-native operations" are related but not specifically targeted at the silent-learning anti-pattern.)
- **Suggested improvement direction:** Add to LONG-ARC "Protected seams" section: "Explicit profile elicitation. Any future profile-learning mechanism must require explicit researcher confirmation per signal addition; implicit learning from behavior is foreclosed by VISION's anti-vision and re-affirmed here."

**E3. "Not arxiv-sanity-but-better" — load-bearing differentiations are mostly post-v0.2.**

- **What:** VISION:81 says "Not arxiv-sanity-but-better. The product overlap is real but the long-arc center of gravity is different (longitudinal memory, multi-lens steering, MCP-native operations)." Of the three differentiators: (a) longitudinal memory is post-v0.2 (per VISION:54 and the absence of a longitudinal-memory subsystem in the v0.2 plan), (b) multi-lens steering ships in v0.2, (c) MCP-native operations ship in v0.1 + v0.2.
- **Why it matters:** Ground 2 (VISION coherence). Two of three differentiators are preserved by v0.2 (b and c). One (a) is not. A researcher actually using v0.2 will see multi-lens steering (real) and MCP operations (real) but will not see longitudinal memory beyond what v0.1 already provides (workflow state, triage history). The "not arxiv-sanity-but-better" claim partly survives v0.2 use, partly survives only on the promise of v0.3+.
- **Severity tier:** quality. Not a v0.2 problem so much as a "what users will perceive after v0.2" calibration issue.
- **Confidence:** medium-high.
- **What would dissolve:** Either (i) clearer scoping of what v0.2 delivers toward the "not arxiv-sanity-but-better" claim (multi-lens + MCP, with longitudinal memory deferred), or (ii) acknowledgment that v0.2 is a *substrate* milestone and the differentiation lives at the substrate layer (lens architecture, citation-graph as first-class data) rather than at the user-facing layer.
- **Suggested improvement direction:** Add to the milestone's Goal section a sentence acknowledging that v0.2 delivers the substrate for the differentiators but not all user-facing manifestations of them — longitudinal memory is post-v0.2.

### Dimension F — Methodology extension scope-creep

**F1. The six practice disciplines are honest lessons, not retrofitted commitments — but two are pre-existing.**

- **What:** METHODOLOGY.md:97-99 frames the practice disciplines as "added 2026-04-25 after the methodology-audit cycle." Looking at the six: A (paired review) and E (pressure-test artifacts) are clearly cycle-derived. B (model verification) is cycle-derived (the explicit Property audit rerun example anchors it). Two (C and D) are best-practice codifications, not new lessons. This is a minor dishonesty about provenance, not a substantive issue.
- **Severity tier:** taste, mostly. Quality if the team relies on the cycle-provenance framing to argue for these disciplines downstream.
- **Confidence:** medium.
- **What would dissolve:** A clarifying note: "Disciplines A, B, E are direct lessons from the 2026-04-25 cycle. Disciplines C and D were pre-existing commitments codified during the cycle. F is a meta-discipline applying to A-E."
- **Suggested improvement direction:** Same as the dissolution. Two-sentence edit to METHODOLOGY.md.

**F2. Adding six practice disciplines did shift the document's center of gravity from interpretive lenses to operational protocols.**

- **What:** METHODOLOGY.md was previously six interpretive lenses (Bayesian, standpoint, paradigm, mechanistic, values-in-science, Duhem-Quine). The practice-disciplines extension adds six operational disciplines. ## Convergent risks

These are places where multiple findings within this audit point at the same underlying weakness. The team should treat these as one issue, not several.

- **Default behavior under multi-value or omitted lens parameter (A1 + C1 + E1's modulus).** The plan proscribes fusion-by-default and silent-defaults at the architectural layer but does not close them at the API contract layer. Three documents disagree on what `lens=` defaults to; multi-value `lens=` is unspecified; the anti-vision against fusion-by-default is preserved at the architectural commitment level but the most-used API surface will pick a default at integration time. The convergent fix is one Phase 13 decision artifact that closes the API contract on (a) default when omitted, (b) behavior on multi-value list, (c) explicit composition parameter shape.
- **Verification discipline applied unevenly across plan claims (D2 + D3).** The Property audit anchored core architectural claims; pre-registered thresholds for the OpenAlex spike are missing; the 126-paper count and `related_works` content presence are unverified. The convergent fix is to extend the property-audit discipline across the v0.2 plan claims systematically, not just the architectural ones — pre-register thresholds for all empirical decisions, verify all factual claims about codebase state.
- **Methodology extension's structural placement (B3 + F1 + F2).** The six practice disciplines are honest lessons and useful guidance, but the placement (extending METHODOLOGY.md rather than its own document), the framing (all six as cycle-derived when two are pre-existing), and the meta-discipline F's distinct status all point at the same underlying issue: the team has methodology pressure to consolidate disciplines into one document and resists the cost of separate-document discipline. The convergent fix is either splitting the document (heavy) or honest frontmatter (light).
- **Anti-pattern recurrence mitigation under-mechanized (A4 + E2 + the absence of a Phase-15-or-16 checkpoint).** The plan correctly identifies the tournament-narrowing recurrence risk and the silent-implicit-learning recurrence risk; both are mitigated by "vigilance" or "deferral" rather than by mechanism. The convergent fix is to add 1-2 phase-boundary checkpoints that operationalize the vigilance — e.g., Phase 15 plan 3 must explicitly check for tournament-narrowing pattern in the lens-extensibility walkthrough; Phase 17 plan 1 must explicitly check that capture schema does not assume lens-of-record-singular.

## 1. Summary

The strongest survived findings, after a slower xhigh pass that re-examined which prior-run claims actually carry their weight:

1. **Default-behavior under-specification at the architectural-commit layer is the single most consequential gap** (`docs/adrs/ADR-0005-multi-lens-v0.2-substrate.md:31`, `.planning/milestones/v0.2-MILESTONE.md:15`, `.planning/ROADMAP.md:282`). Three documents say three slightly different things about what `lens=` defaults to; no document specifies what happens for `lens=[A,B]` without an operation. This is the silent-defaults anti-pattern (`LONG-ARC.md:48`) being reintroduced at the integration boundary by absence rather than by structure. The cross-vendor reviewer flagged it; the prior `high` run flagged it; the disagreement across artifacts is the substantive form.
2. **The Phase 17 "lens-of-record per event" capture schema is the structural recurrence the prior run identified** (`REQUIREMENTS.md:129`, `ROADMAP.md:353`), and the remedy genuinely is one-line — but the deeper finding is that the *capture schema is the analysis pre-commit*: whatever the schema can record is what the pilot can answer, and the pilot replaces the very thing whose framing dissent prompted ADR-0005. This deserves a slightly stronger ground than the prior run gave it.
3. **The OpenAlex coverage spike has no pre-registered decision rule and gates a storage commitment** (`ROADMAP.md:299-303`, `MILESTONE.md:79-86`). Both the prior run and the cross-vendor reviewer named this; what xhigh adds is *what specifically must be pre-registered* (a 3-element decision matrix, not "a threshold"), since "register a threshold" is itself a vague ask that recapitulates the closure pressure being diagnosed.
4. **Critical-path realism: the milestone declares `12 → 13 → 16 → 17` with 14/15 parallel** (`MILESTONE.md:65`), but `ROADMAP.md:333` makes Phase 16 depend on Phases 13 and 15, and Phase 15 depends on Phases 12 and 14. The actual chain is `(12 ∥ 14) → 15; 12 → 13; (13 ∧ 15) → 16 → 17`. This is a coverage-quality, not a doctrine, finding — but it also means "~2 months" rests on a stated path that is not the actual path.

The most consequential strength: the artifact set successfully converts ADR-0005's commit into testable success criteria, traceable requirement codes, and phase gates with specific structural counter-postures (Phase 15 plan 3 third-lens design walkthrough, Phase 16 explicit "not fusion" framing, Phase 17 explicit "no silent profile mutation"). The substrate doctrine is genuinely operationalized at the planning layer, not just at the rhetorical layer. That is what the team committed to do; that is what the team did. Most of my findings below are about whether the *implementation-time* defaults that the planning layer cannot fully specify are governed adequately, not about whether the planning layer is sound.

## 2. Findings by dimension

### Dimension A — Closure pressure recurrence

**A1. "Load-bearing surface" header / opening clause carry framing weight that the body does not fully license** (`VISION.md:34`, `:36`).
- *What.* The header "Load-bearing surface: the multi-lens substrate" and the opening clause "*The architecturally load-bearing claim of this platform is that no single retrieval lens can serve practicing AI research*" do framing work the supporting paragraph supports only partially. The paragraph's three properties (community-structured, methodologically diverse, paradigm-shifting) each motivate adding *a lens type* but do not jointly entail "no single lens can suffice." A sufficiently rich fused embedding could absorb all three properties without contradiction.
- *Why it matters.* Ground 4 (METHODOLOGY discipline D, `METHODOLOGY.md:139-148`): rhetorical labels that do argument-shaping work without supporting argument under them are the diagnosed pattern. VISION is the document the rest of the planning canon escalates to for product identity (`LONG-ARC.md:103`); a load-bearing claim there propagates downstream.
- *Severity.* Quality.
- *Confidence.* Medium. The claim is defensible if reframed as "no single lens has been demonstrated to serve" or as a methodological commitment ("we choose to architect for multiple lenses to avoid premature single-lens commitment"); it is overstated as "*no* single lens *can*."
- *What would dissolve.* Either bridging premises (specific examples of operations a fused single lens demonstrably cannot do — e.g., lens-disagreement *as a signal* requires the lenses to remain separable, which a fused lens by construction destroys), or a register softening to "no single lens we have evidence will suffice" / "the substrate is architected to not depend on a single-lens bet."
- *Suggested improvement.* Add one sentence at `VISION.md:36` specifying that the claim rests on the operational requirement that lens-disagreement, intersection, and per-lens explanation be first-class outputs — operations that fusion-by-construction collapses. That gives the load-bearing label argumentative weight under it.

**A2. Calibration confined to closing/risk sections in three of the four ratifying documents** (`VISION.md:94-112`, `LONG-ARC.md:64-115`, `ADR-0005:48-63`, `MILESTONE.md:84-91`).
- *What.* Each document's load-bearing prose runs declarative through the body; the first hedged language appears at named closing sections (Open Questions, Reopen Conditions, Negative Consequences, Risks). This is the structural pattern METHODOLOGY discipline D names (`METHODOLOGY.md:139-148`).
- *Why it matters.* Ground 4 (the team's own newly-ratified discipline). The prior `high` run made this finding; my xhigh re-examination confirms the *placement* observation but partially walks back the *severity* the prior run assigned. Sectioned hedges are not equivalent to closing-footnote hedges — a "Risks" section reached and read by anyone who reads the document is structurally different from a closing footnote that gets skipped. The discipline-D pattern is most damaging when calibration sits at *document end* in a place readers may not reach. "Risks" / "Open questions" / "Reopen conditions" are reached.
- *Severity.* Quality (downgraded from the prior run's implicit "blocking-adjacent" framing).
- *Confidence.* High on placement observation; medium on whether the placement constitutes a genuine discipline-D violation versus a structural difference between *closing footnotes* (which discipline D names) and *headed risk/open-questions sections*.
- *What would dissolve.* The author could argue that headed sections with semantic load are exactly the *site-of-claim* calibration discipline D wants — "Risks" *is* in-prose hedge for the surrounding architectural commitments. The asymmetry that survives is at specific load-bearing claims (`VISION.md:36-38`'s "no single lens can"; `MILESTONE.md:69`'s "Two lenses ship with structurally distinct retrieval logic" — neither is hedged at the site of the claim).
- *Suggested improvement.* In-prose calibration only at the highest-leverage claims, not throughout: `VISION.md:36-38`, `MILESTONE.md:69`, `ADR-0005:38` ("Cost target: ~2 months"). One hedge each, where the claim is made.

**A3. Multi-item commitments listed without per-item credence** (`MILESTONE.md:14-20`).
- *What.* Seven architectural commitments are presented in identical bullet form. The Property audit (`audits/2026-04-25-phase-3-property-audit-opus.md`) provides direct verification only for the bundle-of-signals primitive. The other six items are inferred consequences ranging from "almost certainly correct given current code" (Lens interface placement, ProfileRankingService refactor target) to "speculative until built" (lens-disagreement and intersection MCP shape). The list does not distinguish.
- *Why it matters.* Ground 1 (stated end goals: the milestone exists to commit v0.2 work). Ground 4 (calibrated language as default register). The reader cannot tell which items are evidentially anchored versus inferred versus speculative — the same closure-pressure pattern the team committed to detect.
- *Severity.* Quality.
- *Confidence.* High that the asymmetry exists. Medium-high that the asymmetry matters at the milestone-document layer (versus being acceptable for an architectural-summary document that defers detail to the ADR).
- *What would dissolve.* The author could argue that `MILESTONE.md:12` ("Architectural commitments (from ADR-0005)") signals the items are commitments, not verified facts, and that mixing verified and speculative commitments is exactly what a forward-looking commitment list does. That argument has merit; it is partially defeated by the absence of any document in the v0.2 set that *does* mark per-item credence.
- *Suggested improvement.* Use the project's own status markers (`CLAUDE.md`: Settled / Chosen for now / Hypothesis / Open) on the seven items in `MILESTONE.md:14-20`. Bundle-of-signals is Settled; Lens interface and dispatcher are Chosen for now (verified-against-codebase as feasible, not yet built); citations storage shape and lens-disagreement MCP shape are Hypothesis. The vocabulary already exists in the project; the milestone does not use it.

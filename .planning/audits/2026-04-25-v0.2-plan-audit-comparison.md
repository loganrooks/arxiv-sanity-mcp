## 2. Audit-unique findings — what each reading caught alone

### Cross-vendor unique (3 findings)

- **#5 critical-path math** — `MILESTONE.md:65` declares `12→13→16→17` with 14/15 parallel; `ROADMAP.md:333` makes Phase 16 depend on 13 *and* 15, and Phase 15 depends on 12 *and* 14. Only the cross-vendor reviewer worked the actual dependency graph against the stated path. The same-vendor audits stayed at the doctrine layer; the cross-vendor reviewer audited the operational artifact against the architectural artifact. (Contaminated xhigh later cited this in convergent risks because it had read the cross-vendor output as a reference.)
- **#18 backward-compat ripple beyond `SearchResult`** — milestone risks named only the `SearchResult` shape change as a backward-compat concern; cross-vendor flagged that Phase 13 also extends CLI flags and MCP tool docstrings, which existing consumers depend on. Same-vendor audits all missed this because they were reading at the architectural-claim layer, not the consumer-impact layer.
- **#24 schema-choice criteria absent** — `ROADMAP.md:300` defers the citations storage choice (normalized edges vs denormalized projection) to Phase 14 with rationale required, but no document specifies what criteria control the choice. Cross-vendor caught this as a coverage-quality issue; same-vendor audits read it as a deferred decision and moved on.
- **#25 Phase 17 reporting tripartite** — cross-vendor recommended the end-of-pilot artifact separate "usage evidence / friction evidence / value hypotheses." Same-vendor audits caught the structural pre-commit problem (#3) but did not propose this analytic structure.

**Pattern:** cross-vendor catches *coverage gaps* and *operational-artifact-vs-architectural-artifact mismatches*. It is reading the artifact set as a coherent specification; same-vendor reads it as a register-and-doctrine surface.

### Same-vendor independent xhigh unique (2 net-new findings)

- **#19 vigilance-is-not-a-mechanism (A4)** — `MILESTONE.md:91` mitigates tournament-narrowing-pattern-recurrence with "LONG-ARC.md anti-pattern watch; rank-and-deprioritize framing throughout." Independent xhigh observed that the team's own diagnosis of the 005-008 drift was that *vigilance was what failed* — each step was locally defensible, the drift was invisible from inside. The mitigation is structurally the same kind of discipline that already failed. Recommendation: convert vigilance into a phase-boundary checkpoint (e.g., Phase 15 plan 3 explicitly checks for tournament-narrowing pattern in the lens-extensibility walkthrough). This is the most consequential net-new finding across the four audits.
- **#23 practice disciplines provenance asymmetry (F1)** — METHODOLOGY frames all six practice disciplines as "added 2026-04-25 after the methodology-audit cycle." Independent xhigh observed that A/B/E are direct cycle lessons; C (factual-claim verification) and D (calibrated language) are pre-existing best practices the team had not codified before; F is meta. Minor provenance honesty issue but a sharper observation than the high run's blanket "F is post-hoc rationalization."

**Pattern:** independent xhigh's net-new findings are both *self-application gaps where the team's own naming exceeds its mechanism*. This is exactly what the same-vendor reading is supposed to catch — the in-house pattern of naming a discipline harder than it operationalizes — and the *independent* condition was load-bearing for both findings (see §3 below).

## 3. Methodology delta — contaminated vs independent

This is the most empirically informative cut. Both runs were dispatched at xhigh effort, with the same subagent role (`adversarial-auditor-xhigh`), the same artifact set, and the same dimensions (A-F). The only material difference: the contaminated run was instructed to treat the prior `high` run as a "baseline for comparison"; the independent run was *forbidden* from reading it.

### What contamination cost (3 documented effects)

**Effect 1: Suppressed discovery of net-new categories.**

The independent xhigh surfaced two findings (#19 anti-pattern-watch-is-not-a-mechanism; #23 practice-disciplines-provenance-asymmetry) that the contaminated xhigh did not. Both findings required the reviewer to re-encounter the documents fresh and notice patterns the prior run did not name. The contaminated run's structure ("Convergence/divergence with the prior `high` run") explicitly oriented the reviewer toward delta-on-prior reasoning, which is finding-extending, not finding-discovering.

**Effect 2: Inherited the prior run's frame even where independent reading sees a strength.**

Finding #17 (`Not ranked-list-but-fancier`) is the cleanest example. The high SV run flagged this as a finding (E2). The contaminated xhigh sustained it as a finding. The independent xhigh judged it a *strength* (E1) preserved by mechanism (Phase 16 lens-disagreement and intersection ops actively preserve multi-lens shape; ADR-0005:36 explicitly proscribes fusion-by-default), modulus the residual risk from finding #2 (multi-value `lens=` default at integration time). The two readings reach opposite conclusions on the same surface; the contaminated reading inherited the prior frame.

This is a category of cost that the convergence matrix does not directly show: contamination doesn't only *miss* findings, it actively *preserves frames* that fresh reading would re-evaluate.

**Effect 3: Findings get softened-from-prior rather than independently calibrated.**

The contaminated run repeatedly used "downgraded from prior run" / "further softened" language (e.g., A2 severity downgrade, B1 further softening). These softenings track what the independent xhigh would have produced anyway, but the *form* is anchored: "this finding the prior run made is partly walked back" rather than "encountering this surface, here is my judgment on its severity." The independent xhigh's calibration is internally generated; the contaminated xhigh's calibration is delta-from-prior. The judgments converge on most items but the reasoning structure differs in ways that matter for downstream synthesis (it is harder to weight a delta-from-prior finding because its calibration depends on the prior, which may itself be wrong).

### What contamination did *not* cost

Contamination did not produce *false* findings or *contradicted* findings. Every finding in the contaminated run is either correct or defensibly debatable; none are wrong-because-anchored. The cost is in *what was missed*, not in *what was wrongly added*.

Contamination also did not slow the audit substantively (both ran in ~6 minutes; contaminated produced 5,400 words to independent's 7,400 — the independent run's longer length tracks the deeper steelman residue and the wider unique-finding surface, not the framing structure).

### What this teaches the team's methodology

The METHODOLOGY discipline A (paired review) prescribes cross-vendor + same-vendor pairing. This comparison demonstrates an unstated *sub-discipline*: **same-vendor adversarial reviews must be dispatched without reference to other audits of the same artifact set**, even other same-vendor audits at lower effort. The "compare to prior baseline" framing has empirical cost in the form documented above.

This is a finding-about-the-methodology, not a finding-about-the-v0.2-plan. It belongs in METHODOLOGY discipline A's "Concrete change" section (`METHODOLOGY.md:112`) as an additional clause:

> **Independent dispatch.** Same-vendor adversarial dispatches must not include other audits of the same artifact set as references, even at lower effort levels. Anchoring on prior findings produces delta-on-prior reasoning, which suppresses net-new finding discovery (~10-15% of findings in the documented case) and inherits prior framings even where independent reading would re-evaluate them.

The synthesis step should consider whether to commit this as a methodology amendment.

## 4. Pair coverage assessment

The METHODOLOGY-prescribed paired review is cross-vendor + same-vendor. With the independent xhigh as the canonical same-vendor pair member, the pair coverage is:

### Joint coverage (both pair members caught)

Findings 1, 4, 13. Three blocking-or-quality findings have multi-vendor agreement; these are robust.

### Cross-vendor only

Findings 5, 18, 24, 25. The cross-vendor reviewer's substance focus catches operational-coverage gaps the same-vendor reading does not surface — particularly *consumer-impact* and *artifact-coherence* findings.

### Same-vendor independent only

Findings 2 (multi-value `lens=` — partly cross-vendor too but only in the joint-with-#1 form), 3, 6-12, 14-17, 19, 23. The same-vendor reading catches *register*, *self-application gaps*, and *anti-pattern recurrence* that the cross-vendor reading does not surface.

### Joint blind spots (neither pair member caught)

This is the hardest category to assess from inside the audit set. Possible candidates, surfaced by inspection of the audit-target artifacts:

- **Phase 14 plan 1's coverage spike implementation details** — neither reviewer asks: is the spike's execution method specified? (E.g., "for each of the 126 papers, query OpenAlex `https://api.openalex.org/works/{doi-or-id}/cited-by`, record edge count + freshness + null-result rate, aggregate.") Without execution-method specification, the spike's results may not be reproducible. *Possible blind spot.*
- **Phase 16's API surface design for lens-disagreement** — the two reviewers caught fusion-by-default risk (#17) but neither audited the design space for the disagreement operation itself (separate tool vs `mode=` parameter vs composable filter — `ROADMAP.md:336` defers this to Phase 16 plan 1 with no criteria). *Possible blind spot.*
- **Whether the v0.1 codebase's existing test suite is sufficient for backward-compat verification of v0.2 changes** — `MILESTONE.md:88` claims `SearchResult` shape change is mitigated by Phase 12 plan 2's regression tests; neither reviewer audited whether v0.1's 403 tests cover the surfaces v0.2 modifies. *Possible blind spot.*

These are speculative; the synthesis step may want to spot-check by reading Phase 14 / Phase 16 / Phase 12 plan files (when they are written) against this list.

### Pair adequacy verdict

The pair is *substantively adequate* for the v0.2 plan as committed. The convergence on findings 1-4 (the blocking surface) is high; the unique-coverage on register and operational-artifact-coherence is genuinely complementary. The pair is *not sufficient* if the test is "did this paired audit catch every finding a third reviewer would catch" — discipline A explicitly notes "the pair is the unit; it is not necessarily the whole." Three speculative blind spots above suggest at least three classes of finding the pair did not surface.

The pragmatic recommendation: synthesize from the pair's findings, do not treat the pair as an exhaustive verdict, and apply phase-PLAN-level paired review (per the original handoff) as Phase 12 / Phase 14 plans are drafted — those will catch what the milestone-level pair did not.

## 5. What this comparison cannot tell you

Bounded scope:

- **Whether any of the 25 substantive findings are *correct*.** This comparison maps which audit caught which finding; it does not adjudicate the finding's substance. The synthesis step is where adoption / deferral / rejection decisions are made.
- **Whether the contamination effects documented in §3 generalize.** This is one comparison of two xhigh runs of one artifact set. The pattern (suppressed net-new discovery; inherited frames; delta-on-prior calibration) is consistent with how anchoring is known to work, but a single comparison is suggestive evidence, not strong evidence. The methodology amendment proposed in §3 should be marked as Hypothesis (per CLAUDE.md status markers), not Settled, until additional audit cycles confirm the pattern.
- **Whether the cross-vendor reviewer's substance focus is empirically substance-deeper than the same-vendor reading.** The cross-vendor caught coverage gaps the same-vendor missed; the same-vendor caught register and self-application gaps the cross-vendor missed. The "substance vs register" division is the dispatch's stated framing; whether it actually holds at the finding level depends on what counts as substance. The cross-vendor's findings #18, #24, #25 are coverage findings, not what most readers would call substance findings.
- **Whether the team should run another audit at a third vendor (e.g., Gemini).** The pair's joint blind spots are speculative; whether a third vendor would catch them is also speculative. The synthesis step may surface specific cases warranting a third reading.
- **Whether the "what works well" sections across the four audits are convergent.** The contaminated and independent xhigh runs both have substantial "what works well" sections; the high SV and cross-vendor runs do not. Comparing strengths-named-across-audits is a possible Section 6 of this comparison; I have omitted it because the strengths converge on similar items (Property-audit-grounded option selection; Phase 15 plan 3 walkthrough; SearchResult backward-compat default; 008 supersession) and the comparison would not add much. The synthesis step should preserve those strengths explicitly.
- **Whether my own framing in this comparison is itself contamination-prone.** I have read all four audits in writing this comparison. My characterizations of "contamination cost" (§3) are themselves potentially shaped by knowing which audit was contaminated. A reader doing their own comparison from scratch might reach different judgments about which findings are net-new vs which are reformulations.

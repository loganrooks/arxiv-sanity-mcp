<decisions_made>

## Architecture Decision
**Parallel views, not fusion.** Three strategies capture three incommensurable kinds of relatedness. The installer should offer them as labeled perspectives:
1. MiniLM → "papers with similar ideas" (semantic precision)
2. TF-IDF → "papers using the same vocabulary" (keyword precision, held-out recovery)
3. SPECTER2 → "papers from adjacent communities" (cross-field discovery, can't rank alone)

## Key Technical Decisions
- float16 storage, float32 search — free 50% memory savings
- K=20 default, K=100+ as exploration mode — smooth tradeoff, user-tunable
- Negative signals: don't implement in v1 — hurts MiniLM, neutral TF-IDF
- SVM eliminated — identical quality to TF-IDF at 10x latency
- BM25 eliminated — worse than TF-IDF on all metrics
- Cross-encoder eliminated — MS MARCO domain mismatch
- Bibliographic coupling eliminated — 19% coverage ceiling, small-sample inflation in prior finding

## Epistemological Findings
- LOO-MRR evaluation is biased toward the embedding model that defined the clusters (confirmed)
- Qualitative review is essential — caught TF-IDF undervaluation that metrics missed
- "Quality" has 3+ incommensurable dimensions — no single ranking captures all
- Strategies should be understood through their character (what they find, what they miss, how they fail), not just their numbers

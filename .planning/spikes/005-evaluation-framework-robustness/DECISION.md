# Spike 005 Decision

## Decision

### Chosen For Now

1. Carry the saved MiniLM-derived family and the `category + lexical` family into Spike 006.
2. Treat Qwen3's candidate-second-view status as **not framework-robust**.
3. Do **not** promote any challenger architecturally on the basis of Spike 005.
4. Keep the full challenger set alive for 006, but with a weaker prior on Qwen3 and without any expectation that profile-family changes alone will produce a new winner.

## Why

- `[artifact-reported]` The strongest family-induced reversal is Qwen3: `candidate complementary second view` under the saved MiniLM family, `blocked / unclear` under both alternatives.
- `[artifact-reported]` No alternative family upgraded any challenger into a stronger positive class.
- `[derived]` The category + lexical family is the best alternative frame to carry because it is more model-independent than the SPECTER2-refined family and still produced material shifts.

## What This Does Not Decide

1. It does not decide that MiniLM-derived profiles are globally trustworthy.
2. It does not decide that Qwen3 lacks value in all workflows.
3. It does not decide that SPECTER2, GTE, or Voyage are poor candidates overall.
4. It does not decide the second-view question for the product.

## Resulting Posture For 006

- `[chosen for now]` 006 becomes a two-frame robustness test:
  - incumbent frame: saved MiniLM-derived profiles
  - carried alternative frame: category + lexical profiles
- `[chosen for now]` The SPECTER2-refined family becomes optional corroboration, not a required execution frame.

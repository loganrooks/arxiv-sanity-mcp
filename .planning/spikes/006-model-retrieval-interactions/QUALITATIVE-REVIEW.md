# Qualitative Review

## Reviewed Cases

This review covers the bounded 10-case interaction surface generated by [phase1_interaction_cases.json](./experiments/review_inputs/phase1_interaction_cases.json). The question is not whether `kNN-per-seed` changes results. It clearly does. The question is whether those changes look like useful retrieval niches or like seed-local fragmentation and adjacency drift.

### `SPECTER2`

- `[artifact-reported]` Under both carried families, `kNN-per-seed` improves the union benchmark relative to centroid and changes the top-20 materially (`mean J@20 centroid-vs-kNN ~= 0.43`).
- `[artifact-reported]` On `P4` and parts of `P2`, the `kNN` results remain recognizably on-topic.
- `[artifact-reported]` On `P6`, `kNN` adds papers like `Generative Semantic Communication: Diffusion Models Beyond Bit Recovery`, which are adjacent but notably less centered on the profile than the centroid list.
- `[derived]` The retrieval shift is real, but it does not read like a clean rescue. `SPECTER2 kNN` looks broader and sometimes more exploratory, but also more seed-local and less disciplined.

### `Stella`

- `[artifact-reported]` `Stella` shows some of the strongest quantitative `kNN` lifts, including `P6` and `P7`.
- `[artifact-reported]` The same cases also show the clearest qualitative instability. `P6` introduces obvious drift such as geosteering, radiograph benchmarking, and unrelated PDE-style diffusion items.
- `[artifact-reported]` On denser-topic cases like `P4` and `P7`, `kNN` remains mostly on-topic and plausibly useful as a niche exploration mode.
- `[derived]` `Stella kNN` is the clearest example of a profile-dependent niche rather than a default improvement. Its gains are real in dense topics and poor in looser ones.

### `Qwen3`

- `[artifact-reported]` `kNN-per-seed` improves the union benchmark for `Qwen3` under both carried families, especially on `P2` and `P4`.
- `[artifact-reported]` The reviewed lists mostly stay within the target topic on `P2` and `P4`.
- `[artifact-reported]` The `P5` case still shows adjacency drift, including a federated-learning privacy paper appearing in a graph-neural-network profile.
- `[source-traceable]` Spike 004 already documented structural vocabulary-sensitive noise for `Qwen3`; see [FINDINGS.md](../004-embedding-model-evaluation/FINDINGS.md).
- `[derived]` `kNN` does not reverse the main concern about `Qwen3`. It changes the shape of the list, but not enough to restore confidence after 005 weakened the family-level story.

### `GTE`

- `[artifact-reported]` `GTE` shows consistent quantitative `kNN` gains without the kind of glaring topical failures seen in `Stella P6` or `Voyage P6`.
- `[artifact-reported]` The reviewed lists remain mostly coherent across `P2`, `P4`, and `P7`, though category recall often falls.
- `[derived]` `GTE` looks like the most controlled instance of method sensitivity: the retrieval change matters, but it widens rather than shatters the underlying topic.

### `Voyage`

- `[artifact-reported]` `Voyage` also shows strong quantitative `kNN` lifts, with notable changes on `P3`, `P5`, and `P6`.
- `[artifact-reported]` Some `kNN` lists remain valuable and on-topic, but others show clear adjacency drift, including speculative decoding in a diffusion profile and quantum-network items in a graph-neural-network profile.
- `[derived]` `Voyage kNN` looks more like a high-variance exploratory mode than a stable improvement. This compounds the already-known API and reproducibility issues rather than relieving them.

## Agreement With Metrics

1. `[derived]` The metrics are right that retrieval choice materially changes the story for every challenger.
2. `[derived]` The metrics are also right that there is no clean winner. In the reviewed cases, `kNN` usually buys breadth or seed-local reach, but rarely in a way that clearly dominates centroid.
3. `[derived]` The review supports treating `Stella`, `SPECTER2`, `GTE`, and `Voyage` as still live after 006, but not because `kNN` solved their problems.

## Disagreement With Metrics

1. `[derived]` Some large union gains look weaker qualitatively than they look quantitatively because the added papers are adjacent rather than central.
2. `[derived]` `Stella`'s quantitative gains overstate how usable `kNN` is outside dense-topic cases.
3. `[derived]` `GTE`'s qualitative picture is somewhat better than a flat `method-sensitive` label suggests; it changes without becoming obviously noisy.

## Bounded Interpretation

- `[derived]` Spike 006 supports `H3`: retrieval method interacts materially with model behavior.
- `[derived]` But the interaction mostly takes the form of **breadth-through-fragmentation risk**, not straightforward quality improvement.
- `[chosen for now]` `kNN-per-seed` should therefore be treated as a profile-dependent exploratory niche, not as the new default retrieval method for any challenger.
- `[chosen for now]` The downstream effect is on shortlist discipline, not on immediate architecture: keep live families that remain interesting after this stress test, but do not let `kNN` alone justify promotion.

## What This Review Cannot Settle

1. `[artifact-reported]` This review covers only the bounded interaction-case set, not every model/profile/method combination.
2. `[derived]` It cannot say whether the broader-but-fragmented `kNN` lists might be more useful in actual research tasks; that belongs to later functional evaluation.
3. `[derived]` It cannot settle whether a method-specific configuration deserves to survive into 008. That requires 007 narrowing or explicit later functional evidence.
